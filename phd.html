Insight into Ph.D. Programmes

Undergraduate Programmes

An undergraduate course is a class or series of classes that students take as part of an undergraduate degree program, typically leading to a Bachelor's degree. The exact structure, content, and duration of an undergraduate course can vary significantly depending on the field of study, the specific program, and the institution.

Short Courses

Short courses are educational programs that typically last from a few days to several weeks. They are designed to provide specific skills or knowledge in a particular area.These courses are designed for individuals who want to learn a new skill or hobby for personal satisfaction.

MPhil and Msc Programmes

Embrace world class gradate education with unique blend of cutting-edge research and industry experience. This is generally a taught postgraduate degree, lasting one to two years full-time (or longer if part-time). The MPhil is often pursued by those intending to continue to a PhD, but it's also a respected degree in its own right.

PhD Programmes

Our PHDs lead the effort to extend the boundaries of knowledge through quality and industry relevant research. A PhD (Doctor of Philosophy) program in computer science is a high-level, research-focused degree that typically takes between 3-5 years to complete, the university, and the specific project.

Ph.D. programmes

Our doctoral program is a comprehensive research program that requires full-time dedication and typically lasts for four years. Successful completion of the program necessitates a high level of commitment from students.To fully engage in the program, students are expected to reside in close proximity to the University and actively participate as full-time members. The postgraduate committee will provide guidance to Ph.D. students, assisting them in creating a customized program of study. This program will encompass a major area of specialization as well as a minimum of two minor areas of specialization.In addition to coursework, the department organizes regular seminars where faculty members and students come together to review and critique research in the field. To encourage constructive critique, students are encouraged to join a research lab and actively participate in the research activities conducted by the group. The department focuses on research areas such as Intelligent Space and Machine Learning, Data Science/Big Data Analytics, Networking and Communications Engineering, and Cyber Security.

Course Description

The course titled "CSCD 721: Grand Research Challenges in Computer Science" focuses on the pervasive presence of wireless communication in contemporary society. It encompasses various aspects of digital mobile wireless networks, offering a comprehensive introduction that combines theoretical foundations with practical applications.Through the course, students will gain a thorough understanding of propagation phenomena, modulation techniques, multiple access schemes, coding techniques, air interface design, wireless network planning and operations, as well as specific technologies like GSM, TDMA, and CDMA. Real-world examples will be used to illustrate the concepts, providing a practical context for learning.Overall, this course aims to equip students with the knowledge and skills required to comprehend and address the significant research challenges associated with digital mobile wireless networks in today's technologically interconnected world.
